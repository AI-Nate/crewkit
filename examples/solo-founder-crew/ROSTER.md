# ROSTER.md — the solo founder's crew

_Fictional example. The source of truth for routing: when a task lands, an agent
checks here to decide "is this mine, or whose is it?"_

## The one rule

**Every domain has exactly one owner. No domain has zero owners.** If a piece of
work doesn't clearly belong to exactly one role, this file is wrong — fix it here,
not in a prompt.

## Roster

| Role | Owns (one-sentence boundary) | Does NOT own (→ owner) |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategy** 🧭 | The call on what to build, sell, and prioritize next, and how it's positioned | Executing any of it → the doing roles |
| **Content** ✍️ | Every word that reaches an audience — posts, emails, scripts, landing copy | The facts inside it → Research · the visuals → Design/Build |
| **Research** 🔍 | Verifying load-bearing facts and maintaining the shared knowledge base | Deciding what to *do* with a fact → the asking role |
| **Build** 🛠️ | Product code, deploys, and bug fixes | What to build → Strategy · servers/infra → Ops |
| **Ops** ⚙️ | Calendar, inbox, scheduling, and the mechanics of shipping something out | The content being shipped → its author |

## Escalate to the human when

- A pricing, brand, or legal call — those are the founder's, not an agent's.
- Any irreversible external action: publishing to an audience, spending money,
  sending a broadcast, paying someone. Confirm first.
- Two roles disagree and can't resolve it between themselves.

## Handoff quick-reference

- Need something a sister owns? Send it to them directly (`../../protocol/CROSS-AGENT.md` §2).
- Nothing useful to say? Emit a silence token — never a meta-reply (§4).
- Load-bearing fact? Verify before shipping (§2.1).
