# Example crew — the solo founder

A **fully fictional** crew for a one-person company: a solo founder who sells an
online course, runs a newsletter, and ships two small products. Five roles, one
human above them. Everything here is made up — no real people, clients, or numbers.

Use it as a starting shape. Copy the folder, rename the roles to your domains,
rewrite the IDENTITY/SOUL bodies. The point isn't these five seats — it's the
*discipline of boundaries* they demonstrate.

## The roster at a glance

| Role | Owns | Hands off to |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategy** 🧭 | What to build/sell next, positioning, prioritization | everyone (sets direction, doesn't execute) |
| **Content** ✍️ | Everything that ships to an audience — posts, emails, scripts | Research (facts), Design (visuals) |
| **Research** 🔍 | Fact-checking, competitive/market lookups, the shared knowledge base | — (service role for the whole crew) |
| **Build** 🛠️ | The products' code, deploys, bug fixes | Ops (infra), Strategy (what to build) |
| **Ops** ⚙️ | Calendar, inbox, publishing/scheduling, the "make it actually go out" layer | whoever owns the content being shipped |

`strategy/` is written out in full as the worked exemplar. The other four are
yours to fill from `../../templates/`.

## A worked handoff — the seam stays invisible

The human asks **Content**: *"Post the launch announcement for the new cohort."*

1. **Content** drafts it — but the cohort's exact start date is a fact it must not
   guess. It sends a one-field ask to **Research**: *"Verify the Fall cohort start
   date — need it for launch copy going out today."* (Fire-and-forget; Content
   keeps drafting the rest in parallel.)
2. **Research** confirms the date against two sources and replies with a `final`
   message. It does **not** post into Content's channel — that would break the seam.
3. **Content**'s next turn boots with the date in context. It drops it into the
   draft, re-reads against its "personal, no hard-sell" voice rule, generates the
   card image, checks the brand color, and posts the finished announcement.
4. Meanwhile **Ops** picks up scheduling it to the newsletter at the right send time.

The human sent **one** message and got **one** finished announcement. They never
saw Content ask Research, never relayed the date, never routed anything. That's the
invisible seam — and it's the entire reason the kit exists.
